Our Mission

The Neighborhood Voice Alliance supports local area groups who are working to preserve and protect their communities.

We believe in protecting the historic character of communities for current residents and future generations.

Above all, we believe in supporting neighbors' efforts to have their voices heard.

How We Achieve Our Mission

  • • Fund and support community groups fighting against overdevelopment and zoning violations
  • • Protect communities from spot zoning that threatens neighborhood character
  • • Support community youth in their personal, civic, and educational development
  • • Preserve essential community character for current and future generations

Currently, we are working in close collaboration with our neighbors in the Charlestown Voice group.

About the Project

The POUA and St. Francis House propose to convert Charlestown's Constitution Inn into housing including 48 units of Permanent Supportive Housing (PSH) for the homeless, including those with physical and mental disabilities such as substance use.

Key Concern: The 48 PSH units will have no requirements for sobriety or psychiatric treatment; it will be "encouraged, but not required."

Community Response

78% of the community does not support the project based on our October 19 call analysis.

Key Concerns Include:

  • • Location suitability
  • • Neighborhood safety
  • • Poor medical care access
  • • Impact on local charities

Official Positions:

  • • City Councilors Murphy, Flaherty, and Coletta have spoken with skepticism and opposition
  • • State Rep. Ryan called it a "bungled process" that should be "taken off the table indefinitely"
